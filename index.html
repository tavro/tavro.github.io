    let's waste some time!

    before my time on the internet, back before search engines were even a thing, people used to list links to fun or useful websites right on their personal webpages. recently, i stumbled upon Aaron Strick's website, and he has something similar that he calls an “outbound” page. i want to have my own version and i call it the “explore” page (just to make things more confusing, he also has an "explore" page, but his is more like a personal blog). on this page, i'll be sharing links to things i find interesting, inspiring, fun, or just stuff that feels like it belongs here. fair warning: this page probably won't be as visually polished or tightly managed as the rest of the site. i might end up using this as a bit of a playground, and the content here will likely get updated frequently.

    this website was written by a human, by hand, using HTML, CSS and JavaScript. no large language models were involved (while i'd like to say “no AI” the term is too broad, and i can't guarantee that AI wasn't involved indirectly — for example, in tools and libraries, or platforms used during development). there's also a plain text only version available if you'd prefer to browse that.
